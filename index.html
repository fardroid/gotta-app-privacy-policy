Analytics that actually changes things.

I'm Farkhad. Twelve years in analytics, most recently as Head of Analytics at Yandex Market. Before that I worked in fintech (uBank) and UGC (Rate&Goods), usually starting as an IC and ending up running the function.

What I like building is teams that don't wait for someone to assign them a question. The good analysts I've worked with notice things on their own, push back on weak briefs, and connect their work to the actual business. That's the standard I try to set.

What I do well.

01

Broad domain experience

Product, marketing, financial analytics, UGC, marketplace. I've moved between domains a lot and tend to pick up new contexts quickly.

02

Analytics that influences decisions

I've spent years working as a partner-analyst to commerce, marketing and finance teams. The goal was rarely just to deliver the number. It was to make sure the right question got asked in the first place.

03

Developing people, not just processes

The analysts I've managed tend to grow fast and take ownership of their areas. I care more about whether they can think through an ambiguous problem than whether they know my favourite SQL pattern.

04

Building from zero

I've set up analytics functions in four business units at Yandex Market and at two earlier companies. The work usually involves a mix of metrics, infrastructure, and changing how people make decisions. The tool stack matters less than getting the basics right.
